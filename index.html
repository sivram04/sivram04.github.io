About

I’m a Business Analytics graduate from the University of New Haven focused on building data systems that turn operational data into structured decision intelligence.

My work sits across the full lifecycle of analytics — from structuring raw data and designing relational models to developing analytical frameworks and reporting systems that support business decisions. I’m particularly interested in how data flows through enterprise processes such as supply chain operations, procurement governance, and operational performance monitoring.

I work with Python, SQL, Snowflake, PostgreSQL, and SQL Server to build automated data pipelines and analytical datasets, and use Power BI and Excel to translate complex analysis into operational dashboards and decision-support reporting. My experience also includes working with enterprise platforms such as SAP S/4HANA procurement workflows and SAP Analytics Cloud for analytics and reporting environments.

My approach focuses on structuring fragmented datasets, improving data reliability, and connecting analytical outputs to measurable operational outcomes. Rather than treating analytics as isolated reports, I aim to design systems where data, models, and business metrics are aligned to support planning, risk visibility, and performance monitoring.

I enjoy working on problems where technical data systems intersect with real business processes—building the infrastructure and analytical frameworks that allow organizations to move from raw data toward consistent, data-driven decision making.
